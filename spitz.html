Spitz

●Spitz are a type of DOMESTIC DOG characterized by long, thick, and often white fur, and pointed ears and muzzles.

●The tail often curls over the dog's back or droops.

■Female - 35–40 cm (14–16 in)

■Coat - Silky texture with curls

■Colour - White, Brown

●Through SELECTIVE BREEDING, spitz have been developed to help humans in three ways: HUNTING, HERDING, and PULLING SLEDS.

Characteristics

●Spitzes are well suited to living in harsh northern climates.

●They often have an INSULATING, waterproof UNDERCOAT that is denser than the topcoat to trap warmth.

●Small ears help to reduce the risk of FROSTBITE, and thick fur that grows on the paws protects the dogs from sharp ice.

●Additionally,

●Spitzes, with their thick fur, fluffy ruffs, curled tails and small muzzles and ears, have been bred into non-working dogs designed to be companions or LAP DOGS.

○Temperament: Spitz dogs are prized for their wonderful temperaments

●These dogs love personal attention, and they won't respond well to being ignored or left alone for long periods of time.

●They are very family-friendly and have a playful spirit.
